How these metrics are calculated
Revenue = Hospitable financials.host.revenue — accommodation + guest fees (cleaning, resort, pet) minus host service fee and discounts. This is what hits your payout, not the gross guest price.
Net Revenue (ex. cleaning) = Revenue minus the cleaning fee charged to the guest. This strips out pass-through cleaning costs to show accommodation-only earnings.
Max Potential (at 95%) = ADR × days in month × units × 95% — a practical target rather than a full sellout ceiling. For a listing with zero bookings in a month, it falls back to the other month's ADR. Use this to see how much is still on the table at today's pricing.
Summary cards = the main headline shows gross revenue against gross potential at 95% occupancy. The smaller subheadline shows your take-home on those same bookings. For owned properties = gross revenue. For managed properties = full cleaning fee + 20% of non-cleaning revenue. Potential scales cleaning proportionally with occupancy. Per-row "your take" appears under Max (95%) on managed listings.
Occupancy % = booked nights ÷ (days in month × units). Multi-unit listings scale the denominator accordingly.
ADR (Average Daily Rate) = Revenue ÷ booked nights.
Attribution = reservations are grouped by check-in month, not by which nights fall in the month.
Data source = the dashboard prefers live Hospitable reservation detail (including financials.host.revenue) from the daily snapshot / Browser Sync. If those reservation financials are missing, it falls back to the Apr 17, 2026 seed for the pacing table and earnings summary.

How the baseline works
For each property, we compute the median days between booking date and check-in across the expanded historical window. Then for the next month, we check what share of nights are already booked vs what your history says should be booked by this point. "Behind" means demand is softer than that listing's usual pace, so pricing or availability may need attention. "Hot" means it is booking materially faster than usual, so the remaining open nights may support a 10% price increase.
